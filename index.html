01 / Automotive Domain · RNT
AI Test Automation Platform
Automating test artifact creation with Generative AI — from manual 50% time spend to standardised, AI-generated outputs
+
Problem

Engineers spent ~50% of their time manually creating test artifacts in Polarion, leading to inconsistency and reduced testing quality

Solution

GenAI-powered platform for automated test artifact generation using LLMs, vector databases, and prompt engineering

Impact

40% reduction in manual effort · 80% improvement in artifact consistency · 100% adoption across Phase 1 teams (India & Germany)

Problem

In automotive testing, engineers were required to manually create highly detailed test artifacts in Polarion for every product increment. The process was time-intensive, repetitive, and inconsistent — teams in India and Germany produced artifacts using different approaches, making cross-team reviews difficult.

  • Engineers spent 50% of their time on documentation, leaving limited capacity for actual validation work
  • Artifacts lacked consistency across teams, increasing review cycles and rework
  • Manual process created bottlenecks that slowed down testing pipelines tied to product safety
Business Context

In automotive testing, test artifact quality directly impacts product safety and reliability. Poor documentation increases the risk of missed edge cases and reduces the overall reliability of testing outcomes. The business case was clear: reducing documentation overhead would free engineers to focus on validation quality while improving consistency across global teams.

Strategy & Hypothesis
If we use Generative AI to automate test artifact creation, we can reduce manual effort by 40–50% while maintaining or improving quality and consistency — allowing engineers to shift focus from documentation to testing.

I validated this hypothesis through structured discovery: interviewed 10 senior test engineers across India and Germany to map the exact pain points, reviewed 3 months of Polarion artifact data to identify high-volume, repeatable artifact types, and confirmed stakeholder appetite for an AI-assisted approach before committing to the MVP scope.

Key Product Decisions
Prioritised GenAI generation over ISO compliance for MVP
Deferring ISO compliance to Phase 2 allowed us to ship a working POC faster, prove value, and secure funding before investing in compliance infrastructure.
Human-in-the-loop validation required before export
Given safety-critical context, all AI outputs required engineer review before Polarion export. This built stakeholder trust and reduced adoption risk.
Leveraged historical artifacts as training context via embeddings
Using existing high-quality artifacts as retrieval context significantly improved output relevance without fine-tuning, reducing time-to-MVP.
Targeted high-volume, repeatable artifact types first
Focusing on the top 3 most common artifact types (covering ~70% of volume) maximised impact while keeping initial scope manageable.

Challenges & Learnings
AI Output Inconsistency
Early outputs were highly variable. I iterated on prompt templates across 4 cycles — adding structured output constraints, adjusting temperature settings, and incorporating few-shot examples from high-quality historical artifacts. This reduced output variance significantly before UAT.
Stakeholder Trust in AI Outputs
Senior engineers were initially skeptical. I ran 6 structured demo sessions using real test scenarios from their own projects, which shifted the conversation from "can AI do this?" to "how do we integrate this into our workflow?"
Data Quality Issues
Historical artifacts varied significantly in quality. I worked with 3 senior test engineers to manually curate a high-quality dataset for embedding context, which measurably improved retrieval relevance.
Future Roadmap
  • Quality guardrails: restrict artifact finalisation to senior engineers for safety-critical types
  • ISO compliance integration for Phase 2 enterprise rollout
  • Continuous feedback loop to improve AI confidence from engineer corrections
  • Expansion to additional artifact types covering remaining 30% of volume
02 / EdTech · HMH
Custom & Premium Reporting Platform
Scaling enterprise reporting through workflow automation — from 3–4 week turnaround to ~1 week, supporting $700K+ in annual revenue
+
Problem

Report delivery took 3–4 weeks due to fully manual SQL + Excel workflows, creating client retention risk on a $700K+ revenue line

Solution

Automated intake and reporting workflow via Salesforce-to-Jira pipeline, standardised report templates, and automated coordination across teams

Impact

60% reduction in manual effort · Delivery time reduced from 3–4 weeks to ~1 week · 15% YoY increase in partner demand

Problem

HMH's Custom & Premium Reports (CPR) product delivered critical student and institutional performance data to US educational districts and state partners. Despite being tied to $700K+ in annual revenue, the entire reporting process was manual from end to end.

  • Reports built using manual SQL queries and Excel workflows, recreated from scratch for each request
  • Delivery timelines of 3–4 weeks per request, causing client dissatisfaction and escalations
  • No standardised intake process — requests arrived through emails, calls, and ad-hoc Slack messages
  • Teams spent the majority of capacity on execution, with no bandwidth for product improvement or new capabilities
Business Context

The CPR product contributed $700K+ annually, with approximately 80% of education partners relying on these reports for data-driven academic decisions. Slow delivery was creating active client retention risk — several Account Managers flagged that partners were exploring alternative data solutions. The business case for automation was both a revenue protection and a growth play.

Discovery & Validation

Before defining the solution, I conducted structured discovery to understand where time was actually being lost. I ran 15 requirement-gathering sessions with Account Managers, mapped the end-to-end reporting workflow across 8 report types, and analysed 3 months of ticket history to identify the highest-volume, most repeatable report categories. This surfaced two key findings: 70% of requests were variations of 4 core report types, and the intake and coordination phases were consuming 40% of total delivery time — more than the actual report-building step.

Strategy & Hypothesis
If we automate the intake, routing, and generation of repeatable report types, we can reduce turnaround time by 60–70% and free up team capacity for higher-value customisation work — without rebuilding the entire reporting infrastructure.
Key Product Decisions
Automate repeatable report types first, not everything
Rather than attempting a full system rebuild, focusing on the top 4 report types (70% of volume) delivered measurable impact in weeks, not months.
Standardise intake via Salesforce rather than building a new tool
Account Managers already lived in Salesforce. Standardising requests there eliminated the coordination overhead of chasing requests across channels.
Process optimisation over headcount increase
Leadership initially considered hiring 2 additional analysts. Automation delivered equivalent capacity increase at lower ongoing cost, with greater scalability.
Automated Jira ticket creation to enforce SLAs
Auto-creating structured tickets with metadata enforced routing logic and created visibility into pipeline status for AMs without manual updates.

Challenges & Learnings
High Customisation Demand
Every Account Manager believed their request was unique. Through data analysis, I demonstrated that 70% of "custom" requests were actually variants of 4 core templates. Setting clear boundaries between standard and truly custom requests was the most important stakeholder alignment conversation of the project.
Manual Process Dependency and Change Resistance
Some team members were comfortable with the existing workflow. I ran phased rollouts — starting with 3 pilot AMs, collecting feedback, and using early wins to build internal advocates before full rollout. This reduced resistance significantly.
Intake Standardisation Across 50+ Stakeholders
Aligning 50+ Account Managers to use a new intake process required more than a training session. I created a concise reference guide, embedded the new process into their existing Salesforce workflow rather than asking them to context-switch, and monitored adoption weekly for the first month.
Future Roadmap
  • AI assistant for Account Managers to pre-fill intake forms using natural language
  • Self-service reporting capabilities for standard report types, reducing engineering dependency entirely
  • Structured request validation to further reduce back-and-forth on incomplete submissions
  • Analytics dashboard for leadership to track pipeline health and SLA adherence in real time
